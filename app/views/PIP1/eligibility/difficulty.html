Difficulty with everyday tasks and getting around

You might get PIP if you find it hard to do certain everyday tasks or get around. This must be due to a long-term physical or mental health condition or disability.

By everyday tasks or getting around we mean:

  • preparing food
  • eating and drinking
  • managing your treatments
  • washing and bathing
  • managing toilet needs or incontinence
  • dressing and undressing
  • talking, listening and understanding
  • reading
  • mixing with other people
  • managing your money
  • planning and following journeys
  • moving around

Do you have difficulty with any of these things due to a long-term health condition or disability?
